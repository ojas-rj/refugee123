About Us

Friends Seeking Refuge is a charity established in the aftermath of the Afghan refugee crisis that seeks to limit the impacts of isolation upon displaced people in Tyne and Wear. Those fleeing their homes to an unfamiliar country often lack familial support, which, when combined with cultural and language barriers, leaves many feeling alone. We aim to combat this - providing a befriending scheme that offers an opportunity for recent arrivals to interact with more established migrants from a similar cultural background.

We match volunteers with individuals to provide social contact and practical support that helps people feel more welcome, connected with the local community, and empowered to regain control of their lives. Befrienders often provide the person they support with local information, helping them to engage with opportunities and facilities.

Volunteers

Families and or individual volunteers are urgently needed, particularly those able to speak the Dari or Pashto language.

Requirements:

  • Reside in or around the Tyne and Wear region

  • Able to offer few hours per month (on average)

  • Able to listen, encourage and support

  • Ideally: bilingual or member of an ethnic minority background
